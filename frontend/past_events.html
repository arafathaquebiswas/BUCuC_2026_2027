বৈশাখী উৎসব ১৪৩২

28 April, 2025, Tuesday
By BUCuC
BRAC University Multipupose Hall & Open Space

Boishakh — a season of light, color, and renewal. BRAC University welcomed Bengali New Year 1432 with a campus transformed into a vibrant canvas of celebration.

The day began with a joyful Boishakhi procession bright smiles, colorful portraits, and hearts full of festive spirit. Under the banyan tree, music and rhythm set the perfect tone, followed by traditional village games and a lively flash mob that spread the energy of Boishakh across campus.

Evening brought an enchanting cultural program — folk songs, graceful dances, and heartfelt recitations reflecting the soul of Bengal. The Boishakhi fair offered traditional foods, handicrafts, and the colors of Bengal in every detail, from sarees and panjabis to intricate alpana designs.

More than just a day, this Boishakh was a heartfelt celebration of culture, heritage, and beauty. The BRAC University Cultural Club warmly thanks everyone who made it truly memorable.

Rythm Revive 24.0

30th December,2024, Monday
By BUCuC
BRAC University Multipurpose Hall & Auditorium

As the curtain closed on the 2023–25 Presidential Panel of BUCuC, we gathered for a two-day celebration filled with nostalgia, artistry, energy, and heartfelt emotion. Rhythm Revive 24.0 was not just a farewell — it was a tribute to creativity, unity, and the unforgettable legacy we built together. Months of rehearsals, planning, and shared dreams culminated in two nights of pure magic.

✨ Day 1: The Show of Hearts — A Journey into Classical Thumri
The first day belonged entirely to our members — a vibrant mosaic of dance, music, drama, and expressive storytelling. The highlight of the evening was a breathtaking 30-minute classical live-music and dance Thumri, a performance crafted from months of passion, persistence, and artistic discipline.

The Thumri Performance brought together lyrical poetry, emotional nuance, and live improvisation — blending classical melody with expressive movement. Our dancers — Nafisa Noor, Shemonti, Poroma, Mitisha, Diana, Arora, Faiza, and Mansura — embodied the essence of longing, devotion, and storytelling through graceful expressions and synchronized choreography.

Behind them, our musicians built the emotional landscape:

Vocal & Harmonium: Avibadhan Das
Tabla: Aparup Chowdhury
Cajon & Darbuka: Ayam Dhar
Benjo: Andalib Mostafa Lalon
Guitar: Almas Ovi, Shafawath Alam
Keyboard: Farhan Ishraq
Dholok: Imtiaz Salam Jami
Bass: Tahmid Rayan

For half an hour, music and movement merged into a shared heartbeat — a farewell gift to our outgoing leaders and a testament to BUCuC’s artistic soul.

✨ Day 2: The Light, Energy & Farewell Concert
Day 2 embraced a vibrant shift — from classical depth to youthful energy. Bands took the stage, filling the night with rhythm and celebration. The grand finale featured beloved artists: Level Five and Indalo, whose performances ignited cheers, nostalgia, and the emotion of closing an era.

Through music and memory, they connected our past, present, and future — reminding us of the journey that shaped us.

A Farewell, A Legacy, A New Beginning
This two-day festival was not just an ending — it was a celebration of dreams fulfilled, challenges overcome, and friendships forged through art. To the Presidential Panel of 2023–25: your leadership guided us toward this unforgettable chapter. And to every performer, musician, dancer, organizer, and volunteer — you transformed vision into reality.

As we turn the page, we welcome a new beginning. May the spirit of Rhythm Revive inspire the next generation to shine even brighter.

Because BUCuC is not just a club — it’s a legacy.

Work. Bond. Glow. ✨

Event Highlights

  • Two-day farewell celebration for the 2023–25 Presidential Panel.
  • Day 1 featured member-exclusive performances across music, dance, and drama.
  • Crowning moment: a 30-minute live classical Thumri performance with dance & live orchestra.
  • Day 2 was a high-energy concert with performances from multiple bands.
  • Grand finale by Level Five and Indalo.
  • A celebration of unity, creativity, and the enduring spirit of BUCuC.

A Magical Qawali Night at AquaVenture: Club Fair Fall ’24 ✨

11th November,2024, Monday
By BUCuC
BRAC University Multipurpose Hall

The BRAC University Multipurpose Hall transformed into a space of pure musical enchantment as BRAC University Cultural Club (BUCuC) presented a soul-stirring Qawali performance at “AquaVenture: Club Fair Fall ’24,” proudly organized by BRAC University Adventure Club (BUAC). What was expected to be a regular club fair showcase soon turned into a transcendent experience—one that left the entire audience mesmerized.

Despite a slight delay, the excitement never faded. In fact, anticipation only grew stronger. Students filled every corner of the hall—standing, sitting on the floor, leaning against the walls—waiting eagerly for BUCuC’s Qawali segment. And the moment the first chord touched the air, the atmosphere shifted. It felt magical.

A Performance That Touched Hearts

The 20-minute performance flowed like a spiritual journey through timeless classics—“Yeh Jo Halka Halka Suroor,” “Kali Kali Zulfon,” and “Kun Faya Kun.” The true magic unfolded when the crowd began to sing along, their voices merging with the performers in a unified chorus filled with devotion, nostalgia, and serenity. For a moment, the entire hall shared the same heartbeat.

What filled the space was more than music—it was peace, warmth, and a sense of togetherness that only heartfelt Qawali can create. Many later said it was one of the most memorable musical moments they had experienced at BRAC University.

The Artists Behind the Magic

BUCuC’s finest performers came together to craft this unforgettable evening:

Main Vocal: Avibadhan Das
Chorus: Meer Abrar Ahmad & Partho
Tabla: Aparup Chowdhury
Kahon: Ayam Niloy
Guitar 1: Meer Abrar Ahmad
Guitar 2: Partho Protim Haldar
Keyboard: Farhan Ishraq
Bass: Sayeed Imam
Percussion: Habib Hasan

Each artist added a unique layer—rich vocals, rhythmic tabla strokes, soothing guitar lines, entrancing percussion, and harmonies that wrapped the hall in mystique. Together, they created a soundscape that felt almost divine.

A Night to Remember

The Qawali night at AquaVenture was more than a performance—it was a shared emotional experience, a celebration of culture, and a beautiful reminder of the power of music to connect and heal. BUCuC once again proved its ability to create magic on stage, leaving every listener touched by the moment.

The hall eventually emptied, but the feeling stayed. For everyone who witnessed it, this night will remain one of the most unforgettable musical highlights of Club Fair Fall ’24.
